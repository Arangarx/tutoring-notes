What the comprehensive view tells you that the matrix can't

The matrix tells you whether a palette + typography pair holds up across one consistent surface, sixteen times. This page tells you something different — whether the pair holds up across different surfaces, where each surface has its own demands. Marketing wants confidence; the dashboard wants density without noise; the recording screen needs to fade into the background so Sarah can teach; the parent view needs to feel like a personal letter, not a transcript.

Three things to specifically check on this F1 × V4w+V2h combination — and a fourth that only matters when you're comparing against F2:

  • Does the green feel "professional tutoring tool" or "outdoor brand"? The brand #1E4D4A is a deep, desaturated teal-green — closer to forest than mint. Scan the marketing hero, the dashboard sidebar header, the "Sessions" tab indicator, and the parent-view dateline. If anywhere it tips toward "REI" or "hiking app" rather than "calm professional tool," that's a signal F2's blue carries more authority at scale.
  • Does coral pop more or less against green? Coral against teal-green is a classic complementary pairing — they energize each other. Look at the parent share view (timestamp pills, play buttons, AI summary tints) and the recording-screen mic ring. Coral may feel warmer here than in F2, because the green's slight warmth meets it halfway. Decide whether that warmth is welcome or distracting.
  • Does the dark mode feel sophisticated or oppressive? F1's dark surface #07201C is a deep teal-forest — moodier and more saturated than F2's near-navy #051A24. The long parent-share prose section is the best place to read at length and decide whether the green-dark feels like a quiet study at night (good) or a closed pine forest (potentially heavy).
  • Side-by-side with F2: open both comprehensive files in two windows. The marketing hero ("Notes that write themselves"), the active recording screen (coral mic ring), and the dashboard "this week" stats are the three highest-signal surfaces for telling the green and blue apart at product scale.

Same as F2: also check that the soft-bold V4 wordmark holds up at the marketing-hero scale, and that Inter 400 body text reads OK in the dense dashboard tables and transcript blocks. Those decisions are palette-independent.
