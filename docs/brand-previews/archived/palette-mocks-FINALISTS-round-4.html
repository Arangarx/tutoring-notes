Forest-Teal Bridge v2, four softness dials. Plus a 2×2 typography matrix.

Two things to evaluate. Color: you and your wife landed on Forest-Teal Bridge v2; the daughter prefers the blues for being "softer on her eyes." She's right — blues at the same lightness read calmer than teals. So this page shows F1 (Forest-Teal Bridge v2 unchanged) plus three softer variants that pull individual dials toward the blue/soft side, ending with F4 which pulls all three dials at once.

Typography: you're torn between V3 and V4 for the wordmark and V2 reads as nicely crisp for the heading. So the typography section is a 2×2 — V3 wordmark vs V4 wordmark crossed with V2 heading vs V3 heading. All four cells render on Forest-Teal Bridge v2 so the comparison stays clean.

What "softer" actually means in F2–F4. Three independent dials: (a) brand color teal→blue undertone, (b) coral accent saturated→muted, and (c) dark-mode surfaces sharper→slightly lifted. F2 turns just dial (a). F3 turns just dial (b). F4 turns all three. Compare F2 to F1 to see "what does bluer alone feel like." Compare F3 to F1 to see "what does muted coral alone feel like."

What F4 is: All three softness dials at once. Bluer brand (F2's contribution), muted coral (F3's contribution), plus dark surfaces lifted from #07201C to #0A2230 (a touch less aggressive black, slightly more breathing room). The most "all sides of the family agree" compromise — daughter gets the blues, you and your wife get the Forest-Teal Bridge structure, the muted coral makes it feel less branded-loud.
vs F1: Whole system feels calmer, less "active." Risk: with all three dials turned, the palette may lose distinctiveness — it starts to feel like a generic "calm SaaS" palette rather than something with the specific Forest-Teal character you bought into. Suggested test: open F1 and F4 side by side and ask "if I had to describe each in 3 words, are they meaningfully different?" If yes, F4's earned the dials. If no, F2 or F3 alone is probably the right amount.
